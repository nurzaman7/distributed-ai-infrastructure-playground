# Repository Naming and Branding

## Candidate Comparison

1. NeuralForge
- Strong: memorable, builder-focused
- Weak: less explicit about distributed systems

2. TraceForge
- Strong: observability-heavy identity
- Weak: may under-communicate model/runtime scope

3. NeuroStack
- Strong: full-stack AI systems signal
- Weak: somewhat generic

4. DeepInfraLab
- Strong: infrastructure + experimental feel
- Weak: less product-like branding

5. InferSys
- Strong: concise, systems-focused
- Weak: less discoverable/SEO-friendly

6. LLMSystemsLab
- Strong: explicit educational positioning
- Weak: may narrow scope to only LLMs

7. OpenTelemetryAI
- Strong: observability alignment
- Weak: appears vendor/standard-bound

8. NeuroScale
- Strong: scaling emphasis
- Weak: does not imply observability/tooling

9. FutureStackAI
- Strong: forward-looking ecosystem branding
- Weak: less explicit on infrastructure depth

10. TitanTrace
- Strong: robust infra + tracing signal
- Weak: tracing-over-indexed perception

11. DistributedMind
- Strong: distributed intelligence identity
- Weak: abstract for infra newcomers

12. HPC-LLM-Lab
- Strong: clear HPC + LLM signal
- Weak: too research-lab in tone

13. AIInfraCore
- Strong: clear infra backbone identity
- Weak: less distinctive brand voice

## Recommended Public Name

**FutureAISystems** (repo can stay `distributed-ai-infrastructure-playground` and brand can be FutureAISystems).

Reason:
- broad scope across LLM infra + observability + optimization
- scalable naming for docs, tutorials, and subprojects
- clear mission signal to hiring managers and contributors
